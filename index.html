Game PLay

Our game consists of three phases: Placement, Prep and Play. These phases will be repeated for a certain amount of rounds or until and objective is completed.



Art Style and Theme

The game is going to have a cyberpunk/futuristic theme akin to movies like Robocop or Blade runner, Characterized by dark, grimy streets, lots of neon, holograms, etc. The art style will compose of a semi real and semi animated style. It will be set in an isometric world view. With that in mind it will be implemented a 3d environment. Both the models and environment will be sculpted with Zbrush and Maya.

Goals

we have several ideas that we might warrant testing once we have a working prototype. One of such idea is that, after a set number of rounds, the game ends and the player with the higher score is declared the winner. The score would be determined by the total gold and experience accumulated by each player throughout the game. Another idea floating around the team is to have a final player-versus-player round. Placement Phase

At the beginning of the phase, each player draws cards to fill their hand. The map then starts off with a single starting square, where the square is the default starting location for each player. The starting square is 1x1, with all four sides having open doors. He starting tile cannot be owned, modified, or enhanced by any player. The players then take turns, with each player placing a single tile on the board. The tile being placed must be matched with a door on the previous tile, and each tile placed is only viewable by the original player and not viewable to other players. There is a time limit to place a tile, if a tile is not placed by the player in the allotted time, then a random tile from their hand will be placed. Once all tiles are placed, then the prep phase begins.

Prep Phase

During the prep phase, players are acting in real time, where upgrades and effects can be purchased. Upgrades can only be purchased for tiles that a player has placed and effects can be purchased for any tile on the map. Some effects can move tiles, in the case where a tile is moved by one player and another player places an effect/upgrade to the tile during the same prep phase the second players effect/upgrade will act on the tile that occupies that location at the end of all moves for that prep phase. In the case that two players move the same tile, either All tile moves are fully processed at the end of the prep phase and in player order or Tile swaps are processed in real time so whoever moves first. Once the prep phase is complete, then the game starts.

Play Phase

In this phase, all players are dropped into their starting location (this may be the default or the starting location moved by an effect. The players then fight their way through the Dungeon they created in real time. When a monster is encountered, fighting a monster earns the players XP and gold. If two or more players damage a monster, then the XOP and gold is split based on the damage done.
